What the PDF reports help you review

The reports pull from what you’ve already logged: sleep, feeding, diapers, and growth. You get a single document organized by date so you can see patterns, share a snapshot with a caregiver, or bring a clear summary to an appointment. The app does not interpret or advise — it only formats your data.

Summary vs full reports

Free: A summary PDF gives you a basic overview of your entries — enough for a quick reference or a short timeline.

Premium: The full Captain’s Log PDF includes detailed daily logs, monthly and yearly statistics, and support for multiple child profiles. Useful when you want a complete record or a longer history in one file.

Organized records for sleep, feeding, diapers, and growth

Everything you log in the app can be included in the report. Sleep times, feeding sessions, diaper changes, and growth measurements appear in one place, ordered by date. No need to copy from multiple apps — Scoopy Log is one log, one export.

Built for clarity, not clutter

The PDF is designed to be easy to read: clear headings, simple layout, and your data front and center. No charts or predictions — just your records in a format you can print, save, or share when you choose.

Common questions

What's in the free PDF?

Free includes a summary PDF with a basic overview of your entries — enough for a quick reference or to share a short timeline.

What's in the full Captain's Log PDF?

Premium adds the full Captain’s Log: detailed daily logs, monthly and yearly statistics, and support for multiple child profiles in one report.

Can I use the PDF for medical visits?

The PDF is your own record of what you logged. You can use it however you like — for personal reference, to share with a caregiver, or to bring to an appointment. Scoopy Log does not provide medical advice.

Is my data private?

Yes. No ads, no tracking, no data selling. Your logs stay on your device or in your optional iCloud account. We don’t have access. The PDF is generated on your device.
